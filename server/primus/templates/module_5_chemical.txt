1. TITLE & DOCUMENT CONTROL
================================================================================
Document Title: Chemical Control & Storage Program
Document Owner: Safety & Compliance Manager
Review Frequency: Annual or upon regulatory changes
Next Review Date: [Review Date]

This program ensures safe handling, storage, and use of chemicals in compliance 
with Primus GFS Module 5.06.

================================================================================
2. PURPOSE / OBJECTIVE
================================================================================
To prevent chemical contamination of food products through:
- Segregated storage away from food products
- Proper labeling and identification
- Approved chemical list for food-contact applications
- Employee training on safe handling and PPE
- SDS accessibility and emergency response

================================================================================
3. SCOPE
================================================================================
Applies to all chemicals used in the facility:
- Cleaning and sanitizing chemicals
- Pesticides and fumigants
- Maintenance chemicals (lubricants, hydraulic fluids, refrigerants)
- Laboratory chemicals and reagents
- Office and administrative chemicals (minimal food safety risk)

================================================================================
4. DEFINITIONS & ABBREVIATIONS
================================================================================
SDS: Safety Data Sheet (formerly MSDS) - hazard communication document
PPE: Personal Protective Equipment (gloves, goggles, aprons, respirators)
Secondary Container: Container other than original manufacturer container (spray bottle, bucket)
Food-Grade Chemical: Chemical approved for food-contact surfaces (NSF, FDA-compliant)
Incompatible Chemicals: Chemicals that react hazardously if mixed (acids/bases, oxidizers/flammables)

================================================================================
5. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================
Safety & Compliance Manager:
- Maintain approved chemical list
- Review and approve new chemical requests
- Ensure SDS accessibility
- Oversee chemical training program

Purchasing:
- Order only approved chemicals
- Obtain SDS before first delivery
- Verify NSF/FDA status for food-contact chemicals

Supervisors:
- Ensure proper chemical use and PPE compliance
- Investigate chemical-related incidents
- Maintain secondary container labeling

Employees:
- Use only approved chemicals per instructions
- Wear required PPE
- Label secondary containers
- Report spills and unsafe conditions immediately

Maintenance:
- Use food-grade lubricants on food equipment
- Store maintenance chemicals separately
- Dispose of waste chemicals per procedure

================================================================================
6. PREREQUISITES & REFERENCE DOCUMENTS
================================================================================
- Primus GFS v4.0 Module 5.06 (Chemical Control & Storage)
- OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200)
- FDA Food Code Chapter 7 (Poisonous or Toxic Materials)
- Approved Chemical List (Form CC-001)
- SDS Binder (online system)
- Spill Response Procedure

================================================================================
7. HAZARD / RISK ANALYSIS
================================================================================
Chemical Hazard: Contamination of food product or food-contact surfaces with 
                 non-food-grade chemicals, cleaning agents, or pesticides
Risk: Acute toxicity, allergen introduction, off-flavors, regulatory violation
Control: Segregated storage, approved chemical list, labeling, training, food-grade 
         chemicals for food-contact use

================================================================================
8. PROCEDURES
================================================================================

8.1 Approved Chemical List
- Approved Chemical List maintained by Safety & Compliance Manager
- List includes: chemical name, manufacturer, use, approval status (NSF, FDA, EPA), SDS date
- Only chemicals on approved list may be purchased and used
- New chemical requests submitted on Chemical Approval Request Form (CC-002)
  * Justification for need
  * SDS provided
  * Alternative evaluation (why existing chemicals inadequate)
  * Food-contact status verified if applicable
- Chemical Approval Committee (Safety, QA, Production) reviews and approves/denies
- Approved chemicals added to list; list updated quarterly

8.2 Chemical Storage
ALL CHEMICALS STORED IN DESIGNATED CHEMICAL STORAGE AREA PHYSICALLY SEPARATED FROM:
- Raw materials and ingredients
- Finished products
- Packaging materials
- Employee break areas and personal belongings

Storage Requirements:
- Chemical storage room/area clearly marked with signage: "CHEMICAL STORAGE - AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY"
- Separate storage for different chemical types:
  * Cleaning/sanitizing chemicals
  * Pesticides (locked cabinet if stored on-site)
  * Maintenance chemicals (lubricants, solvents)
  * Incompatible chemicals segregated (acids away from bases, oxidizers away from flammables)
- Shelving designed for chemical storage (chemically resistant, spill containment)
- Products stored at or below eye level to prevent lifting hazards
- Heavier containers stored on lower shelves
- Secondary containment (trays, pallets) for liquid chemicals to contain spills
- Ventilation adequate to prevent vapor accumulation
- Emergency eyewash/shower accessible within 10 seconds if corrosive chemicals present
- Spill cleanup materials (absorbent, neutralizers, PPE) located in storage area
- Storage area locked when unattended; access restricted to trained personnel
- Inventory log maintained (FIFO rotation to prevent expiration)

8.3 Chemical Labeling
Original Containers:
- Chemicals maintained in original manufacturer containers when possible
- Original labels must be legible and intact
- Damaged labels replaced immediately (use SDS for information)

SECONDARY CONTAINERS (SPRAY BOTTLES, BUCKETS, DISPENSERS) MUST BE LABELED WITH:
- Chemical name (common name or product name)
- Hazard warnings (corrosive, flammable, toxic, irritant)
- Concentration (if diluted from concentrate)
- Date prepared (if diluted solution with limited shelf life)

Label Requirements:
- Waterproof labels or permanent marker
- Legible and durable
- English and primary language of employees
- Labels applied before container first use
- "Water" labeled on water-only spray bottles to avoid confusion

Unlabeled containers:
- Unlabeled secondary containers disposed of immediately (do not attempt to identify contents)
- Corrective action for unlabeled container: employee retraining, supervisor coaching

8.4 Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
- SDS obtained before chemical first use
- SDS maintained in electronic system accessible 24/7 (online SDS management system)
- Printed SDS binder maintained as backup in chemical storage area and main office
- SDS available in English and Spanish (primary languages)
- Employees trained on how to access and interpret SDS
- SDS reviewed during chemical training covering:
  * Hazard identification
  * First aid measures
  * Fire-fighting measures
  * Spill response
  * Safe handling and storage
  * PPE requirements
- SDS updated when new revision received from manufacturer (quarterly SDS update check)

8.5 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- PPE REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENTED FOR EACH CHEMICAL based on SDS recommendations
- PPE matrix posted in chemical storage area and use areas
- Required PPE provided at no cost to employees

Common PPE by chemical type:
- Cleaning/Sanitizing Chemicals: Nitrile gloves, safety goggles, apron (if splash risk)
- Acids/Alkalis (strong): Chemical-resistant gloves, face shield, apron, boots
- Pesticides: As specified on product label (typically gloves, goggles, long sleeves)
- Solvents/Degreasers: Nitrile gloves, safety glasses, ventilation

PPE Inspection & Maintenance:
- PPE inspected before each use for damage (torn gloves, scratched goggles)
- Damaged PPE removed from service and replaced immediately
- Reusable PPE cleaned and sanitized after each use
- PPE storage racks located near use areas

8.6 Chemical Training
ALL PERSONNEL HANDLING CHEMICALS RECEIVE TRAINING BEFORE WORKING WITH CHEMICALS:

Initial Training Topics:
- Hazard Communication Standard overview
- How to access and read SDS
- Chemical hazards in the workplace
- Approved chemical list and proper use
- Safe handling procedures (dilution, mixing, application)
- PPE requirements and proper use
- Secondary container labeling requirements
- Spill response procedures
- Emergency first aid and eyewash/shower locations
- Proper disposal procedures

Hands-On Demonstration:
- Proper dilution technique
- Correct PPE donning and doffing
- Secondary container labeling
- Spill cleanup procedure

Annual Refresher Training:
- All chemical handlers receive annual refresher training
- Training documented on Training Record (Form CC-003)
- Training includes: trainee name, date, topics covered, trainer signature, trainee signature

Job-Specific Training:
- Sanitation staff: cleaning/sanitizing chemicals, concentration verification
- Maintenance: food-grade lubricants, hydraulic fluids, refrigerants
- Pest control coordinator: pesticide storage and record-keeping (if applicable)

8.7 Chemical Use Procedures
Cleaning/Sanitizing Chemicals:
- Follow SSOP for correct product, concentration, contact time, and temperature
- Verify sanitizer concentration using test strips (log results)
- Rinse food-contact surfaces after cleaning if product label requires
- Never mix chemicals unless specifically instructed (e.g., never mix bleach with acids)

Maintenance Chemicals:
- Food-grade (H1) lubricants required for equipment with potential food contact
- Food-grade (3H) release agents for food molds and pans
- Non-food-grade lubricants permitted only on non-food-contact equipment components
- Excess lubricant wiped off to prevent dripping onto product or surfaces
- Lubricant application documented on PM work order

Pesticides:
- Applied only by licensed applicator per Pest Control Program
- If facility personnel apply pesticides (rare), applicator license required
- Pesticide application log maintained (product, site, date, amount, applicator)

8.8 Chemical Spill Response
Minor Spill (< 1 gallon, low hazard):
1. Evacuate immediate area; restrict access
2. Don appropriate PPE (gloves, goggles)
3. Contain spill with absorbent or spill kit materials
4. Absorb liquid using absorbent pads or granular absorbent
5. Place waste in labeled container for disposal
6. Clean area with water or neutralizer as appropriate
7. Dispose of waste per hazardous waste procedure
8. Document spill on Spill Report Form (CC-004)

Major Spill (> 1 gallon, high hazard, uncontrollable):
1. Evacuate area; activate alarm if life safety risk
2. Notify Supervisor and Safety Manager immediately
3. Call emergency responders (911) if life safety risk or environmental release
4. Do not attempt cleanup unless trained and equipped
5. Provide SDS to emergency responders
6. Secure area until professional cleanup completed

Spill Kits:
- Located in chemical storage area, sanitation closet, maintenance shop
- Contents: absorbent pads, granular absorbent, neutralizers, PPE, disposal bags, instructions
- Inspected monthly; replenished after use

8.9 Chemical Inventory Management
- Inventory conducted quarterly (Form CC-005)
- FIFO rotation enforced; oldest stock used first
- Expiration dates checked; expired chemicals disposed of properly
- Minimum/maximum stock levels established to prevent over-accumulation
- Damaged containers identified and repackaged or disposed of
- Inventory records track: chemical name, quantity, date received, expiration, location

8.10 Disposal of Chemicals
- Chemicals disposed of per local, state, and federal regulations
- Empty containers triple-rinsed and recycled (if accepted) or disposed as solid waste
- Hazardous waste (pesticides, solvents, acids/bases) managed per Hazardous Waste Procedure
- Disposal documented: chemical name, quantity, date, method, responsible person
- Disposal records retained 3 years minimum

9. MONITORING PLAN
================================================================================
Activity: Chemical storage inspection
Frequency: Weekly
Responsible: Safety Coordinator
Method: Inspect chemical storage area for proper segregation, labeling, spill containment
Record: Chemical Storage Inspection Log (Form CC-006)
Corrective Action: Correct deficiencies immediately; retrain responsible personnel

Activity: Secondary container labeling verification
Frequency: Daily (by supervisors during floor walk)
Responsible: Department Supervisors
Method: Random check 5 secondary containers; verify labels present and complete
Record: Supervisor Daily Checklist
Corrective Action: Dispose of unlabeled containers; retrain employee

Activity: SDS accessibility check
Frequency: Quarterly
Responsible: Safety Manager
Method: Test online SDS system; verify printed binder current
Record: SDS Audit Log (Form CC-007)
Corrective Action: Update system; replace missing SDS

Activity: PPE compliance observation
Frequency: Daily
Responsible: Supervisors
Method: Observe employees handling chemicals; verify correct PPE use
Record: Supervisor observation notes; coach if non-compliance observed

Activity: Spill kit inspection
Frequency: Monthly
Responsible: Safety Coordinator
Method: Check spill kit contents complete and unexpired
Record: Spill Kit Inspection Log (Form CC-008)
Corrective Action: Replenish missing items within 48 hours

10. VERIFICATION & VALIDATION ACTIVITIES
================================================================================
- Annual internal audit of chemical control program (checklist audit)
- Management review of chemical incidents and near-misses quarterly
- Third-party audit during Primus GFS certification
- Trend analysis: spills, labeling non-conformances, PPE violations
- Employee interviews during audits to assess training effectiveness

Validation Metrics:
- Zero chemical contamination incidents
- Zero chemical-related injuries
- 100% secondary container labeling compliance (audit target)
- 100% SDS availability
- Decreasing trend in chemical-related non-conformances

11. CORRECTIVE & PREVENTIVE ACTION (CAPA) PROTOCOL
================================================================================
Triggers:
- Chemical contamination of product or food-contact surface
- Unlabeled secondary container
- Chemical stored in production area (outside designated storage)
- SDS not available
- PPE not used or used improperly
- Spill incident
- Audit finding related to chemical control

Immediate Containment:
- Remove non-compliant chemical from area
- Segregate potentially contaminated product
- Clean and sanitize affected surfaces
- Provide missing PPE or SDS immediately

Root Cause Analysis:
- Why was procedure not followed? (lack of training, procedure unclear, shortcuts, equipment failure)
- Five Why or Fishbone analysis documented

Corrective Action:
- Retrain personnel on correct procedure
- Clarify procedure if ambiguous
- Provide additional resources (labels, PPE, spill kits)
- Disciplinary action if willful violation

Preventive Action:
- Enhance training materials (visual aids, videos, hands-on practice)
- Increase inspection frequency in high-risk areas
- Improve chemical storage design (better shelving, signage, containment)
- Automate dilution systems to reduce handling

Verification:
- Follow-up observation within 7 days
- Re-audit area within 30 days
- Trend analysis to confirm issue resolved

================================================================================
12. TRACEABILITY & RECALL ELEMENTS
================================================================================
In the event of chemical contamination:
- Identify production lots potentially exposed based on time/location of incident
- Trace-forward to customers if product shipped
- Product disposition: destroy, rework (if feasible and validated), or release with testing
- Disposition decision documented with scientific justification
- Regulatory notification if required (FDA reportable food registry)

Chemical Lot Traceability:
- Cleaning chemical lots recorded on sanitation logs
- Sanitizer concentration logs link chemical lot to production date
- Maintenance lubricant application linked to equipment and production lot via PM records
